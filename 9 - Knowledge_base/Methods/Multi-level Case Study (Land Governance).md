# Multi-level Case Study (Land Governance)

## Description
A **Multi-level Case Study** in land governance involves analyzing a specific policy or program (e.g., land records modernization) across different tiers of the administrative hierarchy (State, District, Mandal/Tehsil, and Village) and through various data sources (official reports, bureaucratic interviews, and grassroots data).

## Strengths / Weaknesses
- **Strengths**: Captures the implementation gap between policy design (state level) and ground reality (village level). Provides a holistic view of the "political economy" of implementation.
- **Weaknesses**: High resource and time requirements. Findings may be state-specific and hard to generalize without comparative cases.

## PhD Relevance
This is a **primary candidate method** for my research into land records modernisation in Odisha. It allows for a deep dive into the bureaucratic processes and the social outcomes of digitization.
