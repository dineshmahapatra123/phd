# New Institutional Economics (NIE)

## Description
A theoretical and empirical approach that builds on the neoclassical model but relaxes the **zero-transaction cost** assumption. It argues that because transacting is costly, **institutions** (defined as the "rules of the game") are the fundamental determinant of economic systems' performance.

## Strengths/Weaknesses
- **Strengths**: Explains the persistence of poverty and growth disparities; bridges the gap between economics, history, and political science.
- **Weaknesses**: Ideology remains a "black box" in early NIE; transaction costs are difficult to directly measure in national accounts.

## PhD Relevance
This is the core methodology of the thesis. It allows for the analysis of land tenure not just as a set of legal rules but as an **institutional bundle** that manages the transition from personal to impersonal exchange in property rights.
