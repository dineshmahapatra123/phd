# Method: Schlager-Ostrom Taxonomy

## Description
A matrix for analyzing property-rights regimes by mapping **five specific rights** (Access, Withdrawal, Management, Exclusion, Alienation) across **five classes of rights holders** (Authorized Entrant, Authorized User, Claimant, Proprietor, Owner).

## Strengths/Weaknesses
- **Strengths**: 
    - Precise identification of who has legal/de facto authority to perform specific actions.
    - Dissolves the binary of "private vs. common" by showing property is a bundle.
    - Enables identifying the efficacy of **[[Proprietors]]** (who can regulate use) vs. **[[Authorized Users]]** (who can only extract).
- **Weaknesses**:
    - Assumes relative clarity in institutional rules.
    - Does not account for "Institutional Bricolage" (where users patch together hybrid rules).

## PhD Relevance
This is your **primary tool** for deconstructing complex land tenure systems (like statutory vs. customary land rights) into measurable units of authority.
