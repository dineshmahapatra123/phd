# People: Armen Alchian


## Scholarly Lens
Armen Alchian is a founding figure of **New Institutional Economics (NIE)**. His core philosophical position views social systems and economic organizations primarily as mechanisms for **resolving conflicts** that arise from resource scarcity. He shifts the focus of economics from "optimal allocation of goods" to the "structure of rights and decision processes."

## Core Bibliography
- **Alchian & Demsetz (1972)**: "Production, Information Costs, and Economic Organization" (Theory of the Firm).
- **Alchian & Demsetz (1973)**: "[[The property right paradigm]]".
- **Alchian (1965)**: "Some Economics of Property Rights".

## Inter-Person Links
- **[[Harold Demsetz]]**: Long-time collaborator at UCLA; together they developed the property rights-based theory of the firm and the broader "paradigm."
- **Ronald Coase**: Alchian built heavily on Coasean transaction cost logic but applied it more broadly to institutional evolution.

## Methodological Bias
- **Comparative Institutional Analysis**: Constantly comparing how different rules (private, communal, state) change the behavior of individuals by altering their incentives and transaction costs.
- **Information Cost Focus**: Heavy emphasis on how the cost of acquiring information and monitoring behavior dictates institutional form.
