# Gershon Feder

**Gershon Feder** is a prominent land economist and formerly of the **World Bank**, best known for his empirical work on the link between **formal land titling** and **economic development**.

## **Scholarly Lens: Empirical Institutionalism**
Feder's work is characterized by the use of rigorous **econometric analysis** to quantify the benefits of secure ownership. He emphasizes two primary mechanisms through which titles translate into growth:
1.  **Tenure Security** (Incentivizing long-term investment).
2.  **Collateral Value** (Facilitating access to institutional credit). 

## **Key Contribution: The Thailand Study (1988)**
In his widely cited study of rural Thailand, Feder and colleagues provided one of the first quantitative benchmarks for the impact of secure titling. They demonstrated that titled farmers invested more, used more inputs, and realized higher revenues than untitled farmers, largely due to the formalization of their access to the credit market.
