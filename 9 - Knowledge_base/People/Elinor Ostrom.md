# Person: Elinor Ostrom

## Scholarly Lens
**Institutional Realism / New Institutional Economics (NIE)**. Ostrom is famous for "Institutional Diversity" and the rejection of simplistic binaries (Private vs. State). She emphasizes **Polycentricity**—the idea that multiple governing authorities at different scales can coexist and interact effectively.

## Core Bibliography
- *Governing the Commons* (1990)
- *Private and common property rights* (2000)
- *Understanding Institutional Diversity* (2005)

## Inter-Person Links
- **Mentor/Partner**: Vincent Ostrom (Public Choice).
- **Adversary (Conceptual)**: **[[Garrett Hardin]]** (challenged his "Tragedy" logic by demonstrating successful de facto communal governance).
- **Collaborator**: **[[Edella Schlager]]** (Bundle of rights taxonomy).
- **Historical Interlocutor**: **[[Henry Sumner Maine]]** (She cites his 1861 work to support the antiquity of communal property).

## Methodological Bias
Uses the **Institutional Analysis and Development (IAD)** framework. Prefers comparative case studies (forests, fisheries, irrigation) and laboratory experiments over pure formal modeling.
