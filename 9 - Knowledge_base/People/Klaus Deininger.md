# Klaus Deininger

**Klaus Deininger** is a Lead Economist in the Development Research Group of the World Bank. He is one of the world's foremost experts on land policy, property rights, and their impact on economic development.

## **Core Arguments**
- **Governance First**: Land administration interventions are not panaceas; their success depends entirely on the underlying governance and policy environment.
- **Low-cost Pragmatism**: Advocates for affordable, participatory land certification (e.g., the Ethiopia model) over expensive, high-precision titling in resource-constrained settings.
- **Market Efficiency**: Emphasizes the role of land rental markets in allocative efficiency and labor mobility.
- **Gendered Tenure**: Highlighted the empowerment effects of granting land rights to women as a driver of household welfare.

## **Key Case Studies**
- **China**: Impact of the 2003 Rural Land Contracting Law on tenure security and labor mobility.
- **Ethiopia**: Success of the large-scale, low-cost certification program.
- **India**: Impact of the computerization of land records (Bhoomi project) on transparency and corruption.
