# People: Harold Demsetz


## Scholarly Lens
Harold Demsetz is central to the **Property Rights School** of economics. His core lens is the **evolutionary theory of property rights**: the idea that property rights emerge when the gains from internalizing externalities (reducing costs of communal use) exceed the costs of establishing and enforcing private rights. 

## Core Bibliography
- **Demsetz (1967)**: "[[Toward a theory of property rights]]" (The seminal paper on rights evolution).
- **Alchian & Demsetz (1973)**: "[[The property right paradigm]]".
- **Demsetz (1968)**: "The Cost of Transacting" (Analysis of market frictions).

## Inter-Person Links
- **[[Armen Alchian]]**: Key collaborator at UCLA.
- **Ronald Coase**: Demsetz refined the "Coase Theorem" by highlighting that identity of owners matters when transaction costs are positive.

## Methodological Bias
- **Evolutionary Logic**: Searches for the "demand" for new institutions based on shifting resource values or technologies.
- **Transaction Cost Analysis**: Focuses on how different rights structures (like toll roads vs. freeways) solve free-rider problems.
