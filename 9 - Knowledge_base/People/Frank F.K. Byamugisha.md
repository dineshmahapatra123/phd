# Frank F.K. Byamugisha

**Frank F.K. Byamugisha** is a Principal Operations Officer at the **World Bank** (East Asia and Pacific Region, Rural Development and Natural Resources Sector Unit), known for extending the Feder et al. (1988) land registration framework from sectoral to economy-wide analysis.

## Scholarly Lens: Economy-Wide Institutionalism
Byamugisha's contribution is the expansion of the land registration–productivity link into a five-channel macroeconomic framework, incorporating financial development as a mediating variable between land registration and growth. He grounds the framework in New Institutional Economics (information and transaction costs theory).

## Core Contribution
The five-linkage framework (1999):
1. Land tenure security → investment incentives
2. Land title → collateral → credit
3. Land liquidity → deposit mobilization → investment
4. Land markets → allocative efficiency
5. Labor mobility → allocative efficiency

## Methodological Approach
Applied the framework empirically to Thailand using time-series econometric analysis. Found: land titling has positive long-run effects on financial development; public expenditure on registration has positive effects on economic growth.
