# Sir Robert Torrens

## Historical Context
**Sir Robert Torrens** (1814–1884) was a British colonial administrator in Australia. He is credited with the creation of the **[[Torrens System]]** of land registration while serving in South Australia.

## Institutional Genesis
Torrens was deeply frustrated by the complexity and "iniquity" of English common-law conveyancing. His personal friend was ruined in the "Maelstrom of the Court of Chancery," which motivated him to find a more efficient system. 

## The Shipping Law Analogy
Drawing from his experience in the customs house, Torrens realized that land titles could be managed exactly like ships. In the shipping industry, a single certificate of title, rather than a chain of historical deeds, was used to transfer ownership efficiently. He adapted the principles of the *English Merchant Shipping Act* to create the **Real Property Act, 1858**.
