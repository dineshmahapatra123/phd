# R. Ramakumar

## Scholarly Lens
Ramakumar R. is a Professor at the School of Development Studies, Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS), Mumbai. His work often adopts a **Political Economy** lens to analyze agrarian change, land relations, and rural development in India. He critically examines the intersection of neoliberal policies and historically evolved agrarian structures.

## Core Bibliography
- **Ramakumar, R., & Ramesh, P. (2023)**. *Illegibly legible: Outcomes of a land records modernisation programme in South India*. Journal of Agrarian Change.
- **Ramakumar, R. (2020)**. *Land records and agricultural statistics in India*.

## Inter-Person Links
- Cites [[D. C. Wadhwa]] regarding land titling committees and the shift to conclusive titling.
- Engages with the work of [[Gershon Feder]] and [[Klaus Deininger]] on land registration and its economic impacts.

## Methodological Bias
- Primarily utilizes **Qualitative Case Studies** and **Secondary Data Analysis**.
- Employs **Semi-structured Interviews** with bureaucratic officials and field-level actors.
- Uses **Political Economy Analysis** to deconstruct policy outcomes.
