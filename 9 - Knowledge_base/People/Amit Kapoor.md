# Amit Kapoor

## Scholarly Lens
**Amit Kapoor** works at the intersection of institutional economics, competitiveness, and governance reform. His research applies competitiveness frameworks to Indian policy problems — particularly land administration, digital governance, and economic development. He is affiliated with both a US-based research university and an Indian policy institute, positioning his work as a bridge between global institutional theory and Indian ground realities.

## Methodological Approach
The 2024 paper uses **comparative policy analysis** — reviewing India's historical evolution of land records, evaluating the DILRMP programme, and benchmarking blockchain/DLT case studies from Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Georgia, Ghana, and Sweden. The analysis is normative and prescriptive, culminating in a governance framework recommendation.

## Core Arguments (2024 Paper)
1. India's land record problem is primarily one of **legal framework**, not technology — digitisation has not addressed the fundamental presumptive titling problem.
2. **DLT** is uniquely suited to land governance because it aligns with the three Torrens principles (mirror, curtain, indemnity).
3. A **governance framework** based on transparency, legitimacy, and accountability is needed before DLT integration — institutional design must precede technology deployment.
4. Incremental, real-time accuracy of land records is more pragmatic than top-down titling legislation.
