# Daniel Bromley

## Biography
Daniel W. Bromley is an institutional economist at the University of Wisconsin, Madison. He is a prominent scholar in the field of natural resource economics and institutional economics, particularly known for his work on property regimes and the "Other Land Reform."

## Key Contributions
- **The Other Land Reform**: Advocates for institutional fixes in the public domain (common property) rather than forced privatization.
- **Taxonomy of Property Regimes**: Defined four distinct regimes: State-property, Private-property, Common-property, and Nonproperty (Open-access).
- **Critique of the Tragedy of the Commons**: Argues that the "tragedy" is a failure of open access, not common property.
