# Birsa Munda

## Scholarly Lens
**Birsa Munda** (1875–1900) was a religious or folk hero and a tribal revolutionary who led the **Ulgulan** (Great Tumult) movement against the British colonial government and the *dikus* (outsiders/landlords) in the late 19th century. He advocated for the restoration of the **[[Khuntkatti]]** system.

## Core Bibliography
- **Ulgulan Movement (1895-1900)**: Lead the armed resistance in the Chotanagpur region.
- **Birsaait Faith**: Founded a new religious sect that combined spiritual purity with political resistance.

## Inter-Person Links
- **Tilak Manjhi**: Predecessor in tribal armed resistance.
- **Sido and Kanhu Murmu**: Santhal leaders whose earlier rebellion paved the way for Munda's resistance.
- **[[Ranjan K. Ghosh]]**: Analyzes Munda's resistance as a catalyst for institutional change (CT Act 1908).
