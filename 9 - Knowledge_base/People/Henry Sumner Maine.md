# Person: Henry Sumner Maine

## Scholarly Lens
**Historical Jurisprudence / Comparative Law**. Maine is known for his thesis that social progress is a movement "from status to contract."

## Core Bibliography
- *Ancient Law* (1861)
- *Village-Communities in the East and West* (1871)

## Inter-Person Links
- **Historical Reference**: Influenced generations of scholars including **[[Elinor Ostrom]]**, who cites his work on Swiss and Indian village communities to argue that communal property is a robust, ancient institution rather than a failed "remnant" of the past.

## Methodological Bias
Uses a **Historical/Comparative method**. Opposed the "occupancy" theory of the origins of property, arguing instead that collective/joint ownership was the archaic form of property.
