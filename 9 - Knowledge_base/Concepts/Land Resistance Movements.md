# Land Resistance Movements

## Standard Definition
**Land Resistance Movements** are organized (armed or non-violent) collectives that oppose state or private encroachment, displacement, or the dismantling of traditional property rights.

## Scholarly Debate
- **Catalyst for Legislation**: Ghosh and Deodhar (2025) argue that resistance often "forces" institutional change. 
    - Santhal Rebellion (1855) → Santhal Pargana Tenancy Act (1876).
    - Munda Ulgulan (1895) → Chotanagpur Tenancy Act (1908).
- **Armed vs. Non-Violent**: 
    - **Tilak Manjhi** (1784): First armed rebel against British land control.
    - **Senapati Bapat** (1921): Mulshi Satyagraha—the world's first recorded organized non-violent struggle against forced displacement (irrigation project).
- **Subaltern Agency**: These movements are not mere "outbreaks" but sophisticated challenges to specific legal instruments like the **Land Acquisition Act (1894)**.

## PhD Application
Useful for analyzing the "Social Feedback Loop" in land governance. It shows how the state's use of **Eminent Domain** is consistently met with countervailing social movements that shape future policy.
