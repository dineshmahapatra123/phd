# Concept: Domain of Demarcated Uses


## Standard Definition
The **Domain of Demarcated Uses** is the socially and legally defined set of permitted actions or "uses" that can be performed with a specific resource.

## Scholarly Debate
- **[[Armen Alchian]] & [[Harold Demsetz]] (1973)**: Use this term to describe the "menu" of possible actions that can be partitioned among different owners. Ownership is defined not by the object, but the *dominance* of an owner's choice over a specific demarcated use.
    - **Partitioning**: Property rights analysis involves identifying how this domain is split. Ownership is absolute when the probability is "1" that the owner's choice dominates the decision process for a particular demarcated use.
    - **Social Construction**: The domain is never fixed; it changes as new uses emerge (e.g., radio frequencies, air rights) or as social prohibitions change (e.g., zoning, drug laws).

## PhD Application
This concept helps in mapping the "legal geography" of a resource—identifying exactly what is permitted and what is prohibited by current institutional frameworks.

## Measurement/Methods
Legal analysis and institutional mapping.
