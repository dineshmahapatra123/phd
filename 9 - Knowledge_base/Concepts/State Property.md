## Standard Definition
A property rights regime where the management of the land is under the authority of the public sector (the state or crown).

## Scholarly Debate
Feder and Feeny (1991) note that many societies constitutionally assign land ownership to the state, while granting exclusive, long-term, and transferable use rights to individuals. If these rights are sufficiently long-term (e.g., 99 years), they become functionally indistinguishable from private property.

## PhD Application
Applicable to India's forest lands (managed by State Forest Departments) and government-owned revenue lands.

## Measurement / Methods
Legal analysis of eminent domain and public land management statutes.
