## 1. Standard Definition
The **Permanent Settlement** (introduced by Lord Cornwallis in 1793) was a land revenue system that recognized Zamindars as the absolute proprietors of the soil and fixed the revenue payable to the state in perpetuity. It was primarily implemented in Bengal, Bihar, and parts of Madras and Orissa.

## 2. Scholarly Debate
While it served the short-term fiscal and political needs of the East India Company by creating a loyal class of landlords, scholars like P.S. Appu and R. Palme Dutt argue it ruined millions of actual cultivators by destroying their customary rights and reducing them to tenants-at-will.

## 3. PhD Application
Essential for understanding the historical context of land governance, agrarian structure, and the roots of modern-day tenancy issues in Eastern India.

## 4. Measurement / Methods
Historical analysis, policy review.
