# Samudaya

## Standard Definition
**Samudaya** refers to common property or collectively managed resources (pastures, grazing lands, forests, water bodies) in ancient and pre-colonial India. These resources were often managed by a village, guild, or community (*Sabha*), rather than individuals or the state.

## Scholarly Debate
- **Sabha Governance**: Access was based on social norms, customs, and community needs, with decisions made collectively by village councils.
- **Ecological Enclosure**: The British declaration of forests and communal lands as "State Property" through various Forest Acts dismantled these traditional systems, turning subsistence practices (grazing, gathering) into illegal acts.
- **Intertwining of Economy & Polity**: Chola-period inscriptions (Uttiramerur) exemplify a "participatory model" where land taxes from *Samudaya* lands funded massive temple and irrigation projects.

## PhD Application
This concept allows for tracking the "Long-Term Erosion of Common Pool Resources (CPR)". It provides a counter-narrative to the idea that land was always privately owned or state-absolutist in India.
