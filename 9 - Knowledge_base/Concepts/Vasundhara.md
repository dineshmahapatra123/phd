# Vasundhara

## Standard Definition
The Sanskrit word for land, literally translating to **"someone that holds wealth"** (Vasundharā). In ancient Indian economic thought, land was conceptualized as the medium or *field of activity* (Kautilya) for wealth creation, rather than wealth itself.

## Scholarly Debate
- **Ashta Lakshmi (8 Wealth Forms)**: Ancient Indian texts specify eight forms of wealth (money, livestock, etc.) but notably exclude land. This suggests land was viewed as a foundational resource for sustenance (Dhana/ grains), not a commodity.
- **The King as Trustee**: Kautilya's *Arthashastra* and Dharmashastras (Jaimini, Narada) emphasize that while the King protects the land, the right of property resides with the householder. The King must often purchase land for endowments, indicating a non-absolutist view of sovereign power.
- **Ecological Continuity**: This view contrasts sharply with the Western/colonial commodification of land into a taxable, tradable asset.

## PhD Application
Useful for contrasting pre-colonial property bundle theories with post-1894 **Eminent Domain** logic. It highlights an "Indigenous Property Right Bundle" that is often erased in mainstream legal histories.
