# Technocracy in Land Administration

## Standard Definition
**Technocracy in Land Administration** refers to the shift of land policy and management from the developmental/political arena to a technocratic sphere governed by experts, automated systems, and efficiency metrics. It implies the depoliticization of land issues, treating them as technical problems of measurement and data management.

## Scholarly Debate
- **[[R. Ramakumar]] and [[Padmini Ramesh]] (2023)**: Argue that technocracy in land administration (as seen in Telangana) fetishizes the need to contain political pressures and legitimizes the autonomy of "experts" and automated portals. This shift often maintains or deepens elite domination by making governmental processes opaque and excluding those without digital access or formal titles (e.g., tenants).
- **General Literature**: Technocracy is often seen as a way to improve the "Ease of Doing Business" by creating predictable, rule-based systems.

## PhD Application
This concept is vital for understanding the **political implications of digital land records**. It challenges the assumption that digitization is a neutral, purely beneficial administrative upgrade.

## Measurement / Methods
- Analyzed through the **abolition of local bureaucratic posts** (like the *patwari*) and the **mandatory use of digital portals** for all land transactions.
