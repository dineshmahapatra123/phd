# Land Market Efficiency

## Standard Definition
The degree to which land can be transferred to its highest-value user at low transaction cost. **Land Market Efficiency** is undermined by asymmetric information between buyers and sellers regarding ownership entitlement. Formal registration systems reduce this uncertainty, enabling more transactions and better allocation of land.

## Scholarly Debate

- **[[Gershon Feder]] & Nishio (1999)**: Identify two mechanisms through which registration improves market efficiency:
    1. **Information symmetry**: Within tight-knit communities, customary systems manage ownership verification well. As markets expand beyond communities, uncertainty over entitlement grows, suppressing demand and offer prices. Registration eliminates this friction.
    2. **Mobility of land**: With clear title, land can move to operators with higher-value skills or investment capacity. Without it, allocative inefficiency persists because potential buyers face legal risk.
- **The Africa Exception**: Migot-Adholla et al. (1991) show that in Ghana, Rwanda, and Kenya, customary systems still provide adequate efficiency for the *current scale* of transactions. Registration becomes efficient only when market demand outpaces customary systems' capacity.

## Empirical Evidence
- **Thailand**: 3–4 years post-titling, land transactions were 35–205% higher in project areas vs non-project areas (Kasetsart University, 1993).
- **Indonesia (Jakarta)**: Registered titles commanded 10.6–28.5% price premium over tax-receipt-only parcels — reflecting the market price of certainty.
- **Peru (De Soto, 1989)**: 69% of Lima houses built in 1985 were in the extra-legal system; buildings on titled lots were worth 9× those on untitled lots.

## PhD Application
Land market efficiency is central to the argument for land registration in India. The suppression of land transactions due to unclear records (Record-Reality Gap) maintains allocative inefficiency and suppresses investment. Connects directly to the McKinsey 1.3% GDP loss estimate ([[Collateral Value of Land]]).
