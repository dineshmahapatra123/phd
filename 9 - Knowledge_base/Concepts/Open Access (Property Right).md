## Standard Definition
A property rights regime where rights are left unassigned (null rights). No individual or group has the right to exclude others, often leading to the "Tragedy of the Commons" and resource degradation due to lack of conservation incentives.

## Scholarly Debate
Feder and Feeny (1991) define this as one of four basic categories of property rights. They distinguish it from [[Communal Rights]], where exclusivity is assigned to a specific group. If a communal group is too large, it may become de facto open access.

## PhD Application
Relevant for analyzing degraded forest lands or unrecorded "wastelands" in India that lack clear institutional protection.

## Measurement / Methods
Bio-economic modeling, satellite imagery of resource degradation, and institutional mapping.
