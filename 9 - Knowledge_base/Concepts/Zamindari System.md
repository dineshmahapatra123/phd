## 1. Standard Definition
The **Zamindari System** is an agrarian structure characterized by the existence of one or more layers of proprietary rights (intermediaries) between the State at the top and the actual tiller of the soil at the bottom. 

## 2. Scholarly Debate
P.S. Appu outlines how this system originated not from deliberate policy but from the decline of the Mughal empire, where revenue farmers and local agents usurped power and were eventually recognized as landlords by the British (e.g. through the Permanent Settlement).

## 3. PhD Application
Crucial for understanding the rationale behind the first wave of Indian land reforms: the Abolition of Intermediary Interests, which aimed to bring the cultivator into direct relationship with the state.
