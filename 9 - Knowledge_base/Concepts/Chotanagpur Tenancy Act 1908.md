# Chotanagpur Tenancy Act 1908

## Standard Definition
The **Chotanagpur Tenancy Act (CNT Act) of 1908** is a seminal piece of protective land legislation in India that restricts the transfer of tribal land to non-tribals. It was enacted by the British colonial government to address the grievances of the Munda and other tribal communities in the Chotanagpur region.

## Scholarly Debate
- **Result of Resistance**: Ghosh and Deodhar (2025) argue that the CNT Act was a direct institutional response to the **[[Ulgulan]]** (Great Tumult) led by **[[Birsa Munda]]**. It represents the "Resistance-Legislation Feedback Loop."
- **Institutional Protection**: By prohibiting land alienation to *dikus* (outsiders), the Act sought to stabilize the region and prevent further uprisings.
- **Legal Pluralism**: The Act formally recognized some aspects of customary land tenure (like **[[Khuntkatti]]**) within the statutory framework of British land law.

## PhD Application
The CNT Act is a primary example of how subaltern resistance movements can successfully force shifts in the **[[Eminent Domain]]** regime. It serves as a historical precedent for modern tribal land protection laws in India (e.g., PESA, Forest Rights Act).
