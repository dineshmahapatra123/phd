# Bhu-Aadhaar (ULPIN)

## Standard Definition
**Bhu-Aadhaar**, formally the **Unique Land Parcel Identification Number (ULPIN)**, is a 14-digit alpha-numeric identifier assigned to every land parcel in India based on its geo-coordinates. Analogous to Aadhaar for individuals, it is a Department of Land Resources (DLR) initiative under the Ministry of Rural Development, implemented within the [[DILRMP]] framework.

## Core Objectives
- **Eliminate duplicates and boundary disputes**: Unique IDs resolve conflicts arising from duplicate land records and unclear parcel boundaries.
- **Enhance accuracy**: Geo-spatial coordinates and digital maps linked to each ULPIN ensure higher accuracy in land records.
- **Streamline transactions**: ULPIN-based records enable faster property transactions with reduced paperwork.
- **Efficient land-use planning**: Comprehensive parcel data supports infrastructure, agriculture, and environmental planning.
- **Empower landowners**: Online access to land records strengthens property rights management.

## Implementation Status (as of 2024)
- Rolled out in **26 states**; implementation ongoing in 9 states.
- **Meghalaya exempted** due to its tradition of community ownership of land parcels — a notable recognition of customary tenure.
- National Conference on 'Bhumi Samvaad IV' (March 2023) focused on ULPIN implementation.
- **9 crore land parcels** assigned Bhu-Aadhaar nationally.
- Target: 100% national coverage by March 2024.
- Transliteration of land records available in 22 languages — addressing linguistic barriers across states.

## Additional DILRMP 2.0 Integrations
- **Aadhaar–land records linkage**: Connecting individual identity (Aadhaar) to land parcel identity (Bhu-Aadhaar).
- **Revenue Court computerisation**: Integration of dispute resolution courts with land records.
- **NGDRS (National Generic Document Registration System)**: Standardised multilingual registration protocol across states.

## Scholarly Debate
### ⚠️ Data Quality Problem
Bhu-Aadhaar assigns a unique ID to existing records — including erroneous ones. If the underlying RoR data is incorrect (as in Gujarat and Tamil Nadu; see [[DILRMP]]), the unique parcel ID legitimises inaccurate information rather than correcting it. The identifier is only as reliable as the data it anchors.

### ⚠️ Meghalaya Exception as Principle
The exemption of Meghalaya for community ownership reasons reveals that ULPIN's individual-parcel logic is structurally incompatible with common property regimes (see [[Communal Rights]]). This is not a marginal edge case — large portions of tribal India operate on collective land arrangements.

## PhD Application
Bhu-Aadhaar represents the foundational data layer required before any [[Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT)]] integration can be attempted — it is the "anchor" that would allow DLT to mirror, track, and verify parcel-level transactions. However, the Meghalaya exception and data quality problems reveal that a one-size-fits-all parcel identity system conflicts with India's plural tenure landscape. Successful DLT integration depends on resolving this upstream problem first.
