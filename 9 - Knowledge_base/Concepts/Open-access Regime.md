# Open-access Regime

## Definition
An **Open-access Regime** is a situation in which there is **no property**. It is characterized by the absence of a defined group of users or owners. In legal terms, it is **res nullius** (nobody's property).

## Key Characteristics (Bromley, 1989)
- **Mutual Privilege**: Every individual has the privilege to use the resource, but no right to exclude others.
- **No Rights**: Since there is no ability to prevent others from using the resource, no one has a "right" in the legal sense (which requires a corresponding duty from others).
- **Rule of Capture**: The resource belongs to no one until it is in someone's possession (e.g., grazing forage or fuelwood).

## The "Tragedy" (Bromley's Critique)
Bromley argues that the "Tragedy of the Commons" (Hardin, 1968) is actually a **Tragedy of Open Access**. The degradation occurs because the "institutional vacuum" assures that use rates will rise until the asset is depleted. The solution is not necessarily privatization, but the conversion of open access into a [[Common-property Regime]].
