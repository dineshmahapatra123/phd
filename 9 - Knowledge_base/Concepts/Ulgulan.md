# Ulgulan

## Standard Definition
**Ulgulan** (meaning "The Great Tumult") is the name given to the tribal uprising led by **[[Birsa Munda]]** between 1895 and 1900 in the Chotanagpur region of modern-day Jharkhand.

## Scholarly Debate
- **Anti-Colonial vs. Anti-Landlord**: The movement was a dual challenge to both British colonial land alienation and the *diku* (landlord/intermediary) exploitation under the **Permanent Settlement (1793)**.
- **Restoration of Khuntkatti**: The primary goal was the restoration of the **[[Khuntkatti]]** (communal ownership) system and the removal of state-imposed forest enclosures.
- **Millenarian Aspect**: Analyzed by K.S. Singh (1983) as a millenarian movement—one that sought a total transformation of the social order based on tribal purity and spiritual guidance.

## PhD Application
The **Ulgulan** is the seminal "counter-colonial moment" in Indian land history. It highlights how the clash between Western **[[Eminent Domain]]** and indigenous property bundles results in protracted conflict and eventual (though often partial) legal reform.
