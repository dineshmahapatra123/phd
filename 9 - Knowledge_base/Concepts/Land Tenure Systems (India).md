# Land Tenure Systems (India)

## Evolution Summary
This concept traces the shifts in ownership and management across three major Indian historical regimes (Ghosh & Deodhar, 2025).

### 1. Ancient/Medieval (Hindu)
- **Vasundhara**: Land as a wealth holder, not wealth itself.
- **Classification**: Rajya (State), Svātva (Private), Samudāya (Common), Devasvām (Temple/Endowment).
- **Hereditary Rights**: Cultivators held secure, hereditary rights as long as they cultivated the land.

### 2. Mughal
- **Revenue Integration**: Land as a source of state revenue.
- **Intermediaries**: Jagirdars (temporary/military) and Zamindars (hereditary revenue collectors).
- **Measurement**: Zabt, Dahsala, and Bigha standardization under Raja Todar Mal.

### 3. British Colonial
- **Permanent Settlement (1793)**: Transferred absolute ownership to Zamindars, transforming land into a marketable commodity.
- **Direct Taxation**: Ryotwari (direct from farmer) and Mahalwari (village-based).
- **Commercialization**: Focus on extractive crops (Opium, Tea).

## PhD Application
The transition from decentralized and community-based governance (Ancient) to centralized and commercialized administration (British) is the foundation of modern land-based asset inequality.
