# Dharani Portal

## Standard Definition
The **Dharani Portal** is an Integrated Land Records Management System (ILRMS) launched by the Telangana government in 2020. It serves as a one-stop portal for land registration and mutation, aiming to provide a transparent and efficient interface for land administration.

## Scholarly Debate
- **Technocratic Vision**: Designed to eliminate the role of the village *patwari* and reduce corruption through automation and centralized control.
- **Critical Critique ([[R. Ramakumar]] & [[Padmini Ramesh]], 2023)**: Characterize the portal as a "dead end" for many farmers. They argue that its rigid design (e.g., requiring total area to match old *sethwar* records exactly) and the abolition of local grievance redressal mechanisms have made the system opaque and inaccessible, leading to hundreds of thousands of unresolved complaints.

## PhD Application
The Dharani portal is an example of **digitization as an instrument of technocratic governance**, relevant for discussions on state capacity and the "digital divide" in rural India.

## Measurement / Methods
- Evaluated through **system runtime errors**, **data entry speeds**, and **grievance redressal rates**.
