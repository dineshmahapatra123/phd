# Khuntkatti

## Standard Definition
**Khuntkatti** is a traditional land tenure system of the Munda tribes in the Chotanagpur region of India. It signifies communal ownership where land belongs to the entire clan (*killi*) that first cleared and cultivated it. 

## Scholarly Debate
- **Communal vs. Individual**: Unlike Western notions of individual property, Khuntkatti emphasizes territorial identity and kinship-based land governance. Rights are inherited within the lineage, reinforcing social cohesion.
- **Colonial Disruption**: The British introduction of the **[[Zamindari System]]** ([[Permanent Settlement]] 1793) broke this communal structure, reclassifying tribal owners as tenants or landless laborers. 
- **The Ulgulan Catalyst**: The systematic alienation of Khuntkatti lands was a primary trigger for the **[[Birsa Munda]]** uprising (1895-1900). This resistance eventually forced the formulation of the **[[Chotanagpur Tenancy Act 1908]]** to offer limited protection to these rights.

## PhD Application
Khuntkatti serves as a critical case study of "Customary vs. Codified" legal conflict. It highlights how the imposition of the doctrine of **Eminent Domain** and state property (Forest Acts) commodified indigenous livelihoods.
