# Concept: Common-pool resources (CPR)

## Standard Definition
A **Common-pool resource** is a type of economic good characterized by two key attributes:
1. **High Cost of Exclusion**: It is difficult or costly to exclude potential beneficiaries from using the resource (via physical or legal barriers).
2. **High Subtractability**: Use or consumption of resource units by one individual subtracts from the quantity available to others.

## Scholarly Debate
- **Ostrom (2000)**: Carefully distinguishes CPRs (the *resource type*) from Common Property (the *property regime*). A CPR can be governed as private property, state property, or common property.
- **Confusion with Public Goods**: CPRs share "difficult exclusion" with public goods but differ because they are subtractive (public goods are non-subtractive).
- **Confusion with Open Access**: Neoclassical economics historically conflated the *resource attribute* (CPR) with the *lack of rules* (Open Access), leading to the "Tragedy of the Commons" prediction.

## PhD Application
Foundational for identifying resources (like groundwater or grazing lands) that require institutional design beyond simple individualization/privatization.

## Measurement/Methods
Analyzed using the **[[Schlager-Ostrom Taxonomy]]** to map who holds which rights over the resource system vs. resource units.
