# Rythu Bandhu Scheme

## Standard Definition
The **Rythu Bandhu Scheme** (Farmer’s Investment Support Scheme) is a welfare program launched by the Government of Telangana in 2018. It provides a direct cash grant to farmers (initially ₹4,000 per acre per season, later increased) to support agricultural investments such as seeds, fertilizers, and labor.

## Scholarly Debate
- **State claims**: Lauded as a pioneering direct benefit transfer (DBT) scheme that bypasses middlemen and provides timely support to farmers.
- **Critical Perspective ([[R. Ramakumar]] & [[Padmini Ramesh]], 2023)**: Argue that the scheme is "regressive" because it is based on landownership rather than actual cultivation. Since tenants are not recorded in the official RoRs (due to the [[LRUP (Telangana)]] exclusions), they are entirely excluded from the scheme's benefits, while absentee landlords receive large subsidies.

## PhD Application
The Rythu Bandhu Scheme is a key example of how **land records serve as the gateway to social welfare**, and how inaccuracies or exclusions in records lead to systemic inequality.

## Measurement / Methods
- Evaluated through **disbursement data**, **landholding size distributions**, and **tenant exclusion rates**.
