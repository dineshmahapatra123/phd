# Concept: Attenuated Property Rights

**Attentuated Property Rights** occur when the **[[Property Right Bundle]]** associated with a resource is incomplete or constrained by institutional rules (Alchian & Demsetz, 1973).

## **Standard Definition**
In its simplest form, property rights are attenuated when the state or a third party prohibits some uses that would otherwise be desired by the owner.
- **Rent Control**: An attenuation where the owner's right to set a market-clearing price is restricted.
- **Price Capping**: Limits the ability of the owner to capture the full exchange value of their rights.

## **Impact: Transaction Costs & Allocation**
- **Non-Price Allocation**: When rights are attenuated (e.g. price and profit are capped), the **price mechanism** is blocked. Allocation then shifts to non-price methods: queues, favoritism, and discrimination (Alchian & Demsetz, 1973).
- **Inefficient Use**: Monitoring and enforcement costs rise as owners and users attempt to circumvent the attenuation.

## **PhD Application**
This concept is critical for analyzing the **Record-Reality Gap** in Indian land records. Colonial and post-colonial land laws often "attenuated" the bundle by creating complex layered interests (Zabt, Jagirs, Zamindaris), where no single party held the "full" bundle, leading to incessant litigation and stunted evolutionary growth.
