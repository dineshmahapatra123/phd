# LRUP (Telangana)

## Standard Definition
The **Land Records Updation Programme (LRUP)** was a state-wide initiative launched by the Telangana government in September 2017. Its primary objective was a "special time-bound, purification and updation of land records" to create an accurate database of landholders.

## Scholarly Debate
- **State Claims**: The government claimed the programme was a major success, increasing the "purity" of land records from 65% to 93%.
- **Critical Perspective ([[R. Ramakumar]] & [[Padmini Ramesh]], 2023)**: Argue that LRUP was driven by the political need to identify beneficiaries for the [[Rythu Bandhu Scheme]] (cash transfers) rather than a genuine attempt at agrarian reform. They highlight that the programme relied on updating textual records without a fresh physical resurvey, leading to the reproduction of historical errors and the exclusion of tenants.

## PhD Application
LRUP serves as a contemporary case study of **[[Implementation Failure (Land Reform)]]** and the limitations of technocratic solutions in complex political economy environments.

## Measurement / Methods
- **Process**: Conducted in two parts (Part A for undisputed records, Part B for disputed ones).
- **Outcomes**: Measured through the number of new Patta Passbooks (PPBs) issued and the percentage of cleared agricultural khatas.
