# Bundle of Rights

A conceptual framework in property law, championed by Wesley Hohfeld and A.M. Honoré. It defines property not as a physical object, but as a complex bundle of legal relations (rights, privileges, powers, and immunities) between people regarding an object. This view allows the state to unbundle and regulate specific rights without violating 'ownership'.
