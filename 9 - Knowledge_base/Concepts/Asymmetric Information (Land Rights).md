## Standard Definition
A situation where transacting parties (buyers/sellers or lenders/borrowers) have unequal knowledge regarding the validity of land rights, boundaries, or encumbrances. This asymmetry increases transaction costs and risk, potentially leading to market failure or segmentation.

## Scholarly Debate
Feder and Feeny (1991) emphasize that as agricultural development proceeds and transactions occur between non-local parties, asymmetric information increases. Public [[Institutional Arrangements]] like land records and titles are developed specifically to reduce this asymmetry and expand the scope of the land market.

## PhD Application
The core justification for moving from presumptive title (high asymmetry) to conclusive title (low asymmetry) in the Indian land record modernization program.

## Measurement / Methods
Analysis of land dispute incidence, transaction costs, and credit market segmentation.
