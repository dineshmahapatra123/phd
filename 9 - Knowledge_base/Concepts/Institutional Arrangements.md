## Standard Definition
The specific laws, regulations, associations, contracts, and property rights regimes created within the framework of the [[Constitutional Order]]. These arrangements govern the interactions between agents and the allocation of resources.

## Scholarly Debate
Feder and Feeny (1991) emphasize that institutional arrangements (like land titling systems) can be more readily modified than the constitutional order, but their effectiveness depends on congruence with cultural [[Normative Behavioral Codes]].

## PhD Application
The primary layer for analyzing land registration systems (conclusive vs. presumptive title) as specific institutional arrangements designed to reduce transaction costs.

## Measurement / Methods
Analysis of specific legislation, regulatory frameworks, and contractual patterns.
