**Simon Smith** @\_simonsmith [2025-12-27](https://x.com/_simonsmith/status/2004999842678837368)

I’m 90% confident that by the end of 2026, mainstream AI tools will bring the power of Claude Code to all users, but take it even further. They will:

1\. Proactively suggest creating new apps and features, and other useful deliverables, each day. Not wait to be told what to do.

2\. Upon approval, create a plan and delegate tasks to sub-agents.

3\. Use the appropriate Skills and MCPs for each sub-agent to execute its step.

4\. Have a supervisor agent review the outputs and ensure quality.

5\. Reach out to users for help when needed, via channels like Slack and text message that humans regularly use.

6\. Display their progress on a dashboard.

You will give feedback. That feedback will get processed overnight into new proactive recommendations for deliverables to review and approve.

You will find yourself slowly becoming a manager of agents. It won’t be exactly clear when the transition happened from being an individual contributor empowered by an AI assistant to being a manager of a team of agents. But one day, you’ll realize it happened.

You’ll still chat with AI. But the majority of tokens you pay for won’t be in response to your questions or requests. They’ll be part of an AI-driven sequence from proactive recommendation through to multi-step execution.

All the pieces are already in place. ChatGPT Pulse makes proactive recommendations, Claude Code and Codex plan and execute multi-step tasks using Skills and MCPs, Anthropic is working on a task mode interface to track agentic task progress.

By the end of 2026, this will all mature. Everything will be part of a seamless, user-friendly tool that changes the way mainstream users interact with AI.
