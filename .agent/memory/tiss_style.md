Apply these rules automatically when drafting or editing any PhD chapter text in this vault. Full ruleset is at `9 - Knowledge_base/TISS_STYLE.md`.

**Why:** User ingested the TISS Manual of Style (Jayaram 2019, 8th ed.) and wants all writing to comply without having to prompt each time.

**How to apply:** Before writing or editing any sentence of chapter prose, mentally run through this checklist:

- UK English spelling: *-ise* not *-ize*, *-our* not *-or*, *per cent* not *percent* or *%*
- Numbers: spell out 1–9; numerals for 10+; never open a sentence with a numeral
- Oxford comma in all lists of three or more
- Short quotes in single quotation marks (' '); long quotes (≥ 3 lines) block-indented, no marks
- Author-date citations: *(Sharma 2005)* — no comma; page with colon *(Sharma 2005: 34)*
- No *i.e.*, *e.g.*, *etc.* in body text — write *that is*, *for example*, *and so on*
- First paragraph of every section: NO indent. Subsequent paragraphs: 21.6pt indent
- En-dash for ranges (*2000–2010*), em-dash for parenthetical breaks (no spaces)
- Active voice and first person preferred
- *&* only in parenthetical citations; *and* in all prose
