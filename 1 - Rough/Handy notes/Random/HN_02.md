Sure! Here is the "Explain Like I'm 10" version of how I build this automation for you.

Imagine I am a chef (the AI), and I have a kitchen robot helper (the Browser Subagent).

### 1. The Recipe (The Workflow)

First, I need to give the robot a very specific set of instructions. Robots are fast but dumb—if I don't tell them exactly what to do, they get stuck.

- **My Instruction:** "Go to X.com. Wait 5 seconds. Then, spin the mouse wheel down. Wait 1 second. Do that 40 times."
- **Why?** Because X.com is an "infinite scroll" website. The tweets don't exist until you scroll down to "reveal" them.

### 2. The Robot Goes to Work (The Subagent)

I send the robot into the browser. You won't see me typing; you'll just see the browser moving.

- It goes to the website.
- It "pretends" to be a human. It sends a signal to the browser saying "Mouse Wheel Down," just like your finger would.
- It waits. If it doesn't wait, the website won't have time to load the new tweets.
- It repeats this over and over until it has a huge long page of loaded tweets.

### 3. The "Magic Camera" (The Extraction)

Once the robot has scrolled enough, it uses a special trick. It doesn't "read" the screen with eyes. It looks at the **code** behind the website (the HTML).

- I give the robot a special pair of glasses (JavaScript code).
- These glasses look for every box that is labeled "Tweet".
- Inside that box, it grabs:
    - The **Name** (e.g., @ElonMusk)
    - The **Text** ("Doge is cool")
    - The **Link** (x.com/status/123...)
- It writes all of this down in a big list (a JSON file).

### 4. The Chef Cooks (The Analysis)

The robot comes back to me and says, "Here is the list of 100 tweets I found!"

- Now _I_ (the smart AI) look at the list.
- You asked for "Last 24 Hours Only." So, I check the time on each one.
    - "2 hours ago" -> **Keep.**
    - "2 days ago" -> **Throw away.**
- You asked for "No junk."
    - "Ad for Shoes" -> **Throw away.**
- I take the good ones and write them nicely into a file for you.

### Summary

1. **Plan:** I tell the robot what to do.
2. **Scroll:** The robot opens the page and scrolls to load data.
3. **Scrape:** The robot grabs the text from the code.
4. **Filter:** I clean up the list and give you the final report.
